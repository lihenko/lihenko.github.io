What we don’t do and why

Managed legal services and general legal services

Kenios is not an SRA regulated law firm, but we do provide the services of SRA regulated solicitors directly to our clients.

The SRA permits SRA regulated solicitors to provide non-reserved legal services directly to clients through an organisation which is not regulated by them. The solicitor will still be personally regulated by the SRA as a result. All our solicitors do hold current practicing certificates and all the work which they undertake is non-reserved in nature.

Please be aware that SRA authorisation and regulation does not apply to Kenios itself, to the services that Kenios offers more widely (i.e. where not being provided by a specific solicitor) or to work carried out by those in the business who are not also solicitors.

Neither Kenios nor any Kenios lawyer holds client money. Where this service is required, we partner with a Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) regulated payment services institution. This is a huge advantage as such institution leverages its payments domain competence ensuring the secure and compliant handling of funds. Regulatory oversight ensures that these institutions maintain the highest standards of security, confidentiality, and accountability, thereby extending an additional layer of trust and assurance to our clients.

Professional indemnity cover

Solicitors working for Kenios are not subject to the SRA's compulsory professional indemnity insurance requirements. Kenios does maintain professional indemnity insurance which covers our solicitors’ services, however, the terms of this insurance are not the same as the SRA minimum terms and conditions of professional indemnity insurance. The SRA's compulsory levels of insurance drive overhead which ultimately manifests in increased fees in other firms. We believe that our PI cover is more proportionate to the work we do and we are able to pass the associated savings onto our clients. Should any client wish to increase the level of PI cover we are of course happy to do so at additional cost. Please refer to our client engagement letter for further information.

Complaints about our solicitors

In the unlikely event that you need to make a complaint about one of our SRA regulated solicitors, we have a comprehensive complaints procedure which is available on request and is also set out in our client engagement letter.

If you are not satisfied with the result of any complaint, or if we do not resolve any such complaint within eight weeks, our clients have the right to take any complaint about the standard of service offered by that solicitor to the Legal Ombudsman. If it upholds a complaint the Legal Ombudsman has a range of options available to it, including ordering a solicitor to reduce a bill or to pay compensation. Kenios guarantees to stand by any final decision of the Legal Ombudsman in that regard.

This complaints procedure covers SRA regulated solicitors only and the SRA and the Legal Ombudsman have no power or jurisdiction in relation to Kenios itself.

General

Kenios is not covered the SRA Compensation Fund. Whilst the compensation fund is a discretionary fund in any event, which means that no-one is entitled to a payment, solicitors working for Kenios are not covered by this fund meaning that anyone receiving services from Kenios lawyers are not entitled to make a claim against this fund regardless of any other eligibility criteria.

Why are we not SRA regulated? We believe it is a “no brainer”

We are owned and operated by solicitors, but Kenios has chosen not to apply for SRA regulation. We have the upmost admiration and respect for the SRA but we do not provide any services to the public which are reserved legal services (or any other such services requiring SRA regulation). Therefore, there is no requirement for us, as a firm, to be regulated.

With SRA regulation there is a significant cost burden for the company. Please see this link for guidance on the SRA’s requirements. Where regulation is required, this increased cost of regulation absolutely makes sense. Where regulation is not required, our view is that the significant costs benefit of being self-regulated should be passed on to the client. Please see our pricing page for further details.

Whilst the company itself is not regulated, we are still able to provide SRA regulated solicitors directly to our clients and this allows us to offer first class regulated professionals at a significant discount to traditional law firms without affecting the quality of the service or the trust that our clients have in the integrity and capability of our lawyers.

Reserved legal activity

Where a client requires a legal professional to carry out reserved legal activities, either as an adjunct to work we are handling or otherwise, we have a partner SRA regulated firm and we happily recommend our clients to them when required, on a zero-obligation basis. Further details on these arrangements are available when required.

Legal resourcing

Our model covers both short and long term resourcing. Pricing will vary depending on the length of assignment, nature of the work and skill set required. We find that we are able to cover these assignments at a cost often in excess of 50% cheaper than a traditional law firm would charge.
