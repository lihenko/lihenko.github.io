WELCOME TO 188 HENRYK LANE, MONROE TOWNSHIP PA 18612, USA

Welcome to a distinguished and historic estate nestled in the picturesque Back-Mountain region of Dallas, PA. The farmstead, established in 1865, is a testament to American heritage, proudly listed on the National Register Program by the Bureau for Historic Preservation in Harrisburg. Its origins trace back to the Civil War Draft Registration of 1863, marking it as a cherished American landmark. This self-sustaining haven is strategically located a mere two hours from the bustling metropolises of New York and Philadelphia, offering an idyllic retreat without straying far from urban conveniences. The estate's past is rich with agricultural vigor, once teeming with livestock and bountiful crops The property is adorned with fieldstone walls that gracefully delineate its various pastures, adding to its rustic charm and character. Spanning 50 acres, this historic farm estate is enveloped by a serene landscape, with enchanting waterfalls and meandering creeks enhancing its tranquil ambiance. It stands as an unparalleled setting for leisure and enjoyment, promising a lifestyle of privacy and beauty. Whether you're drawn to the allure of living on a gentleman's farm or seeking a unique lifestyle experience, the farm offers the perfect canvas to realize your dreams in an undisturbed paradise.

Your Convenient Escape from NYC and Philadelphia

Escape the hustle and bustle of city life and find your sanctuary at 188 Henryk Lane. Conveniently located within a short distance from both New York City and Philadelphia, this property offers the perfect retreat for those seeking tranquility and convenience.

View on map
Distance from Major Cities:

Situated just two hours away from both New York City and Philadelphia, getting to 188 Henryk Lane is a breeze. Take I-80 W or Turnpike 476 N, and you'll find yourself immersed in the serene beauty of this idyllic location in no time.

Historic Significance:

Nestled in Pennsylvania's historic Wyoming Valley, 188 Henryk Lane holds a special place in American history. It was here, amidst the picturesque landscapes, that the Battle of Wyoming took place during the American Revolution.

Cultural Connection:

Immerse yourself in history and culture as you explore the famed Wyoming Valley, a region steeped in rich heritage and significance. From the battlegrounds of the American Revolution to cultural landmarks like the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City, the surrounding area offers a wealth of experiences waiting to be discovered.
