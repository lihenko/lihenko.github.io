Frequently Asked Questions

Aerodynamic grain cleaners use controlled air flow to efficiently remove dust, chaff, and light impurities from grain streams. In addition, they enable separation of grain by specific weight, allowing lighter, underdeveloped kernels to be removed and thereby improving seed quality and supporting better germination performance.
Rotary drum grain cleaners are designed for efficient grain cleaning and separation by size. The rotating perforated drum ensures continuous movement of the material and extended contact with a larger screening surface, which improves separation accuracy and throughput.
Scalpers (vibro-sieves) operate by using vibration of the sieve to separate grain and impurities by size. As the material moves across the vibrating screening surface, oversized particles are retained while properly sized grain passes through the openings, ensuring efficient and continuous size-based separation.
Sieves of various mesh sizes are used to separate grains by size, remove foreign particles, and ensure uniform quality.
Seed treaters apply protective coatings to seeds, while conveyors transport grain efficiently between machines without contamination.
Most grain cleaners are designed to process dry grain. Increased moisture content reduces machine productivity and separation efficiency, therefore pre-drying is recommended to ensure optimal performance and consistent cleaning results.
Air-based separation systems and optimized drum designs reduce power consumption while maintaining high cleaning performance.
Maintenance is straightforward: clean sieves, inspect drums, lubricate moving parts periodically, and replace worn components.
Spare parts and technical support are available through authorized distributors or directly from the manufacturer to ensure longevity and efficiency.
