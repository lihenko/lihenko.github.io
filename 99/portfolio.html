Before showing you what we do, let us tell you how we do it. Our bold promise is “the Quality of an Agency for the price of a Freelancer”.
You might be asking yourself what differentiates us from a random, untrained, freelancer. Here’s the answer.

Our team has got almost a decade of experience creating Facebook Ads, we’ve been through all the stages of the platform and we adapted to each and every of them.

The current one starts with the infamous iOS update, and ever since then we’ve seen creatives taking over.

To an external viewer, it might seem that designing creatives is not that hard. Hire a graphic designer, tell them what you like, and that’s pretty much it.

However, this would lead us to enormous amounts of money wasted in opportunity cost and underperforming ads.

What we want to do is approach creative design as a science, and make sure that all tests have a specific purpose and lead to new learnings and insights.

How do we do it?

Introducing our Client’s Deck:

Our clients on monthly retainers have access to this, and it literally allows you to run broad campaigns and still get results (as long as the rest is in the right place).

As veterans in the advertising field, it’s hard to admit it but nowadays creatives play the biggest role when it comes to results.

Let’s go through our client’s deck. As you can see, we start writing down the last update, the type of content tested and the preview link.

Once that’s done, we move on to the Copy Number (what copy has been tested alongside with the visual creative), Funnel Phase (or level of awareness), Subtitles, Stage (has it been tested, is it being tested or it has not been tested yet) and Result (Winner, Half-Winner or Loser).

Then we have our notes, basically our conclusions and what we’ve learned from that specific test. RIght after, we have the link to the KPIs on the Business Manager.

Finally, we have the test score. The score is based on 6 main factors, such as:

  • Attention Score
  • CTR Link/Interest Score
  • ATC/Desire Score
  • IC Score
  • Purchase Score
  • ROAS/Action Score

All these factors can be scored from 1 to 5 and the sum of them give us the total score. Based on the total score, we will be prioritizing our future tests.

Imagine how impactful this process could be to your company's success. No freelancer will bring this to the table, and our pricing structure makes it affordable to literally everybody.
